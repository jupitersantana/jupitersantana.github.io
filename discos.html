About Discos

These flour-based discs, called "discos," have been available since the 1970s and offer convenience and good taste. They're commonly used in various foods such as empanadas, stuffed pockets, twists, and other pastries. The empanada discos are popular worldwide and are served in many establishments such as restaurants, cafeterias, food trucks, and catering services. They can be filled with a variety of ingredients, including meat, vegetables and fruit, and are available in different sizes. These empanada disc doughs are made with natural ingredients, are trans fat-free, and contain no artificial preservatives or additives. They can be prepared in either an oven or deep-fried.

From Discos to Empanadas

See the easy process to make empanadas using Cubanita Frozen Foods' discos with our illustration below.

1. Defrost the Disco (dough shell) package for 1 hour at room temperature.

2. Place the thawed stack on a flat surface or equivalent, and gently peel off a single Disco (dough shell) from the stack.

3. Select the desired filling (about 1-2 oz) and make sure to orientate it in the center of the disco while lightly moistening the edges with water.

4. Carefully hold onto the edge of the Disco (dough shell) and turn the dough over, forming a half-circle or half-moon shape. Close the sides by gently pressing down on the folded dough with fingers and/or thumbs around the filling bulge.

5. Finish by crimping about a 3/4-inch width size. A fork end is commonly used by simply pressing down along the folded dough edge to assure a tight seal closure. Then, cook according to the instructions below. Make as many as you wish, then store for future use."
